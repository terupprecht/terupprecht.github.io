Projects

Predictive Modeling of Marine Microbial Abundances - Comparing Multiple Machine Learning Modeling Strategies

This master’s thesis investigated how well the abundance of microorganisms can be predicted with the help of various models using different data sets and types (satellite data, zooplankton biomass measurements and amplicon sequencing data from various expeditions). Five modeling approaches (including random forest and neural networks, each with hyperparameter tuning) were compared, with particular attention paid to the spatial correlation of the data in the model evaluation.

Predicting future bark beetle infestations in the Bavarian Forest National Park

Consulting project as a group (3 people) in collaboration with the Bavarian Forest National Park with the goal of predicting future bark beetle infestations. A random forest and a logistic regression model were used as base models for the performance evaluation of the convolutional neural network applied.

Prediction of the number of covid patients in the ICU at district level from May 2020 to October 2022 through a Feed Forward Backpropagation Neural Network

As part of the seminar "Introduction to statistical analysis of areal data": Modeling and evaluation of a neural network with hyperparameter tuning to predict the number of Covid patients in intensive care units.

Triggering-Prozesse seismischer Aktivität - Fortgeschrittene Regressionsmodelle zur Analyse der Nachbebenproduktivität

[Triggering processes of seismic activity - Advanced regression models analyzing aftershock productivity]

Investigation of aftershock productivity in New Zealand as part of the bachelor thesis using various regression models (generalized additive models, Poisson/quasi-Poisson/negative binomial model).

Vom ländlichen Populismus zu den Wahlerfolgen der NSDAP: Welche Rolle spielten die Bauernparteien?

[From rural populism to the electoral success of the NSDAP: What role did the farmers' parties play?]

In cooperation with the Geschwister-Scholl-Institute for Political Science (Teamwork): Analysis of the correlation between the losses of the farmers’ parties and the gains of the NSDAP using a quasi-binomial model with random intercept.
